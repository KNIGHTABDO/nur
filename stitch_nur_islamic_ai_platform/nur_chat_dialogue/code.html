What is the ruling on missing Fajr prayer intentionally?

spa
verified Answer

Intentionally missing the Fajr prayer is a major sin in Islam. It is farḍ (obligatory), and abandoning it deliberately is a grave matter.

menu_book

Allah ﷻ says:

إِنَّ الصَّلَاةَ كَانَتْ عَلَى الْمُؤْمِنِينَ كِتَابًا مَّوْقُوتًا

"Indeed, prayer has been decreed upon the believers a decree of specified times."

— (Quran 4:103)

The Prophet ﷺ said:

مَنْ تَرَكَ صَلَاةَ الْعَصْرِ فَقَدْ حَبِطَ عَمَلُهُ

"Whoever leaves the 'Asr prayer, his deeds are annulled." (Often cited by scholars regarding intentional abandonment of any obligatory prayer).

— (Sahih al-Bukhari)

Scholars are agreed that leaving it intentionally makes one sinful and worthy of severe warning. Repentance (Tawbah) and making up the prayer (Qada) are generally required.
